Schooling in Rural Parts of Nepal

About this map

Schools in rural parts of Nepal often face significant challenges due to geographic isolation, limited resources, and socio-economic factors. Many schools are located in remote hilly or mountainous areas, making access difficult for both students and teachers, especially during the monsoon season when roads become impassable. Facilities in these schools are often inadequate, with poorly constructed buildings, lack of furniture, and insufficient teaching materials. Electricity and internet connectivity are scarce, hindering modern learning opportunities. Despite these obstacles, local communities and organizations have made efforts to improve education through initiatives like teacher training, scholarships for underprivileged students, and partnerships with NGOs.
